# Maria Pepe and the Fight for Equality in Sports

These Young Democrats hate belonged to Maria Pepe. She was a twelve-year-old girl from New Jersey who became a symbol of the fight for equality in sports during the 1970s. In 1972, Pepe joined a Little League baseball team. League officials ordered her removal becuase she was a girl. Her experience became part of a larger legal and social battle over whether girls should have the same opportunities as boys in athletics.

With support for the National Organization for Women (NOW), Pepe challenged Little League's policy.  At the time, many people believed that girls shouldn’t participate in competitive sports with boys. They also believe that athletics were a male activity. These beliefs reflected those seen in education, careers, and public life. They limited opportunities for women becuase it was ‘for men.’ By standing up against discrimination, Pepe became a huge example of how young women challenged traditional gender roles.

Her case ultimately helped lead to a 1974 ruling that required Little League Baseball to allow girls to participate. This opened opportunities for thousands of girls across the country and showed that athletic ability should not be determined by gender. Although Maria Pepe was only a child, her actions contributed to a large movement for equality.
