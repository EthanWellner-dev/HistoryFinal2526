# Title IX 

Title IX was signed into law in 19722 as a part of the Education Amendments Act. This law is one of the most important pieces of legislation in the history of women's sports. The law prevents inequality and discrimination based on sex under any education program. That being said, Title IX was not written specifically for sports, but it drastically transformed opportunities for women in sports.
	
Before Title IX, female athletes had significantly fewer opportunities than male athletes. Many schools offered limited sports programs for females, offered fewer scholarships, and provided inadequate facilities. Before Title IX, many females played a modified version of basketball known as six-on-six basketball. Under these rules, players were restricted to either offense or defense and would have to stay on that respective side of the court. They could not run the full length of the court. This was because people believed women weren't as capable as men to play five-on-five basketball. After Title IX was passed, this was changed; women could now play five-on-five basketball.

Title IX represents a major victory in the fight for equality and shows how the women’s rights movement created opportunities that continue to benefit female athletes today. Without Title IX, many of the opportunities available to women in sports today would not exist.
